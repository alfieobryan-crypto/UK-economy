About this data: “Irregular migration” refers to people entering the UK outside official routes. The Home Office publishes small boat crossing figures and asylum statistics separately from ONS migration estimates. Channel crossing figures only capture one route — most irregular entrants arrive by other means (lorries, ferries, visa overstays). Asylum grant rates reflect initial Home Office decisions and exclude appeals, which overturn a significant proportion of refusals. Asylum backlog figures are estimates based on published quarterly data.
